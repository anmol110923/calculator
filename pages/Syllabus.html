Year 1 Syllabus

All resources are structured, trusted, and updated regularly for IPU

All your syllabus, one click away!

  • Applied Chemistry +
    • Unit 1: Fuels +
      • Classification & Characteristics of fuels
      • Calorific values: Definition & comparison (solid/liquid/gaseous fuels)
      • Determination of calorific values: Bomb calorimeter, Boy's calorimeter
      • Theoretical calculation: Dulong formula (with numericals)
      • Solid Fuels: Coal (proximate & ultimate analysis), carbonization (Otto-Hoffman oven), metallurgical coke
      • Liquid Fuels: Petroleum (mining, refining, knocking), combustion numericals
    • Unit 2: Engineering Materials +
      • Portland Cement: Rotary Kiln manufacturing, gypsum role, setting & hardening chemistry
      • Glass: Tank furnace manufacturing, annealing, types (soft/hard/borosilicate)
      • Polymers:
        • Basics: Classification, functionality
        • Applications: Polyethylene, PMMA, nylon, bakelite, polycarbonate
        • Special polymers: Conducting, liquid crystalline, biodegradable
    • Unit 3: Water +
      • Water quality standards & characteristics (physical/chemical/biological)
      • Hardness: Disadvantages, EDTA method, numericals
      • Alkalinity: Numericals
      • Boiler Problems: Scale/sludge, corrosion, caustic embrittlement, priming/foaming
      • Treatment:
        • Internal: Carbonate/phosphate, Calgon conditioning
        • External: Lime-soda, zeolite, ion-exchange (with numericals)
      • Desalination: Reverse osmosis, electrodialysis
    • Unit 4: Corrosion & Control +
      • Definition, effects, mechanisms (dry/chemical, wet/electrochemical)
      • Pilling-Bedworth ratio
      • Types: Galvanic, pitting, stress, differential aeration, soil corrosion
      • Passivity & influencing factors
      • Protection: Galvanization, cathodic/sacrificial anodic, electroplating
  • Programming in C +
    • Unit 1: Introduction to Programming & C Basics +
      • Computer Systems: Components, computing environments, languages
      • Program Lifecycle: Preprocessor, compilation, linker, execution
      • Algorithms: Flowcharts, pseudocode
      • C Language:
        • History, program structure, compilation process
        • Tokens: Keywords, identifiers, constants, strings, variables
        • Data types, I/O statements, type conversion
      • Operators & Expressions:
        • Arithmetic, relational, logical, bitwise, conditional operators
        • Precedence, associativity, evaluation, type conversions
    • Unit 2: Control Structures, Arrays & Functions +
      • Control Structures:
        • Decision: if, switch
        • Loops: while, for, do-while
        • Jump: break, continue, goto
      • Arrays:
        • 1D/2D/multi-D: Declaration, initialization, accessing
      • Functions:
        • User-defined vs built-in, storage classes
        • Parameter passing: Call by value, arrays (call by reference)
        • Recursion
      • Strings:
        • Character arrays, variable-length strings
        • Library functions: strlen(), strcpy(), etc.
    • Unit 3: Pointers, Structures & File Handling +
      • Pointers:
        • Basics, arithmetic, pointer-to-pointer, generic pointers
        • Arrays of pointers, dynamic memory allocation
        • Pointers to functions, strings
      • Structures & Unions:
        • Definition, initialization, nesting, arrays of structures
        • Unions, typedef, enumerations
      • File Handling:
        • Command-line arguments, file modes (r/w/a)
        • Operations: fopen(), fclose(), etc.
        • Multi-file programming
    • Unit 4: C Libraries & Algorithms +
      • C99 Extensions
      • Standard Libraries:
        • stdio.h, stdlib.h, math.h
        • string.h, time.h, etc.
      • Basic Algorithms:
        • Factorial, Fibonacci series
        • Searching: Linear, binary
        • Sorting: Bubble, insertion, selection
        • Square root, array/string reversal
  • Communication Skills +
    • Unit 1: Fundamentals of Communication +
      • Introduction: Role, importance, attributes
      • Types of Communication:
        • Verbal: Skills, clarity, tone
        • Non-verbal: Body language, gestures, facial expressions
      • Barriers:
        • Socio-psychological, intercultural
        • Strategies to overcome barriers
      • Effective Communication: 5Cs (Correctness, Clarity, Conciseness, Courtesy)
      • Group Communication:
        • Meetings: Types, purpose, conduct
        • Group discussions, presentations
    • Unit 2: Spoken English & Etiquette +
      • Spoken vs Written English: Formal vs informal, Indian/British/American variations
      • Etiquette:
        • Personal behavior, greetings, introductions
        • Telephone etiquette
      • Vocabulary Development:
        • Dictionaries, thesaurus
        • Commonly confused words, one-word substitutions
        • Comprehension exercises
    • Unit 3: Professional Writing & Speaking +
      • Letter Writing:
        • Planning: Message, content, structure
        • Types: Enquiries, quotations, bargaining, complaints
        • Memos, circulars, notices
      • Paragraph & Report Writing:
        • Scientific/technical reports: Types, structure
      • Speech Drafting:
        • Audience analysis, text preparation
        • Types: Commemorative, welcome, farewell, condolence
    • Unit 4: Grammar & Advanced Concepts +
      • Grammar:
        • Articles: Definite, indefinite
        • Tenses: Present/past/future perfect, conditionals
        • Active/passive voice, direct/indirect speech
        • Degrees of comparison
      • Common Errors: Usage mistakes, corrections
      • Listening Skills: Active listening, comprehension
  • Applied Physics 1 +
    • Unit 1: Thermodynamics +
      • Fundamentals:
        • System, state, equilibrium, process
        • Continuum model, equations of state
      • Laws of Thermodynamics:
        • Zeroth Law: Thermal equilibrium
        • First Law: Energy conservation, work, heat
        • Second Law: Entropy, irreversibility
    • Unit 2: Waves & Electromagnetic Theory +
      • Waves & Oscillations:
        • Simple harmonic motion, wave equation
        • Superposition principle
      • Electromagnetism:
        • Maxwell’s equations
        • Poynting’s theorem, energy/momentum in EM fields
        • EM waves: Wave equation, plane waves, energy transfer
    • Unit 3: Optics +
      • Interference:
        • Division of wavefront: Young’s double slit, Fresnel’s biprism
        • Division of amplitude: Thin films, Newton’s rings, Michelson interferometer
        • Coherence
      • Diffraction:
        • Fraunhofer vs Fresnel diffraction
        • Single/double/N-slit (grating), circular aperture
        • Resolving power (Rayleigh criterion), dispersive power
      • Polarization:
        • Brewster’s law, Malus’ law
        • Nicol prism, double refraction
        • Wave plates (λ/4, λ/2), optical activity, polarimeter
    • Unit 4: Modern Physics +
      • Relativity:
        • Michelson-Morley experiment, speed of light
        • Galilean/Lorentz transformations
        • Time dilation, length contraction, mass-energy equivalence
        • Invariance of Maxwell’s equations
      • Laser Physics:
        • Coherence, Einstein coefficients (A & B)
        • Population inversion, laser operation
        • Examples: He-Ne laser, Ruby laser
  • Manufacturing Process +
    • Unit 1: Introduction & Metal Casting +
      • Manufacturing Basics:
        • Definition, importance, classification
        • Material properties
      • Metal Casting:
        • Sand Casting: Moulds, patterns (types, materials, allowances)
        • Moulding sand: Types, properties
        • Core making, gating system
        • Cupola Furnace: Operation
        • Special Casting: Shell, die, centrifugal casting
        • Casting defects
    • Unit 2: Joining Processes +
      • Welding Fundamentals:
        • Principles, classification
        • Fusion welding: Gas welding (equipment, filler/flux)
      • Arc Welding:
        • Electric arc, MIG, TIG, submerged arc, electro-slag
        • Resistance welding
      • Welding defects
    • Unit 3: Deformation Processes +
      • Metal Forming:
        • Hot vs cold working
      • Forging:
        • Open/closed die forging, typical operations
      • Rolling: Principles, types
      • Drawing & Extrusion:
        • Rod/wire/tube drawing
        • Extrusion: Hot/cold, types
      • Sheet Metal:
        • Shearing, bending, drawing
        • Stretch forming, metal spinning
    • Unit 4: Powder Metallurgy & Plastics +
      • Powder Metallurgy:
        • Process: Powder production, blending, compaction, sintering
      • Plastics Manufacturing:
        • Thermoplastics: Injection, blow, rotational moulding, extrusion
        • Thermosets: Compression, transfer moulding
        • Bonding techniques
  • Electrical Science +
    • Unit 1: DC Circuits & Analysis +
      • Fundamentals:
        • Passive components (R, L, C)
        • Basic laws (Ohm's Law, temperature resistance coefficients)
        • Voltage/current sources
      • Circuit Analysis:
        • Series/parallel circuits, power/energy calculations
        • Kirchhoff’s Laws (KVL, KCL)
        • Nodal & Mesh analysis
      • Network Theorems:
        • Delta-star transformation
        • Superposition, Thevenin’s, Norton’s theorems
        • Maximum power transfer theorem
      • Time Domain Analysis: First-order RC/LC circuits
    • Unit 2: AC Circuits & Power +
      • AC Fundamentals:
        • Sinusoidal waveforms (peak, RMS values)
        • Phasor representation
      • Power Concepts:
        • Real/reactive/apparent power
        • Power factor
      • AC Circuit Analysis:
        • R, L, C, RL, RC, RLC combinations (series/parallel)
        • Resonance
      • Three-Phase Systems:
        • Balanced circuits
        • Star/delta connections (voltage/current relations)
    • Unit 3: DC & AC Machines +
      • DC Machines:
        • Generators & motors: Principles of operation
        • Speed control (shunt/series motors): Flux, rheostatic, voltage control
      • AC Machines:
        • Generators & motors: Principles, revolving magnetic field
        • Induction motors: Squirrel cage/phase-wound rotors
        • Starters: DOL, star-delta
        • Synchronous machines
    • Unit 4: Transformers & Instruments +
      • Transformers:
        • Construction, working principle, equivalent circuit
        • Losses, regulation, efficiency
        • Auto-transformers, three-phase connections
      • Measuring Instruments:
        • Electromagnetism principles
        • Moving Iron: Attraction/repulsion types
        • Moving Coil: PMMC, dynamometer types
  • Applied Mathematics 1 +
    • Unit 1: Multivariable Calculus +
      • Partial Differentiation:
        • Chain rule, implicit differentiation
        • Exact differentials
      • Optimization:
        • Maxima, minima, saddle points
        • Lagrange multipliers
      • Advanced Topics:
        • Differentiation under integral sign
        • Jacobians, coordinate transformations
    • Unit 2: Ordinary Differential Equations +
      • First-Order ODEs:
        • Geometric interpretation, direction fields
        • Euler's method, separable equations
        • Exact ODEs, integrating factors
        • Linear ODEs, Bernoulli equation
        • Applications: Population dynamics, orthogonal trajectories
      • Higher-Order ODEs:
        • Homogeneous linear ODEs with constant coefficients
        • Euler-Cauchy equations
        • Wronskian, nonhomogeneous ODEs
        • Variation of parameters
      • Series Solutions:
        • Power series method
        • Legendre's equation and polynomials
        • Bessel's equation and functions (Jn, Yn)
        • Gamma function
    • Unit 3: Linear Algebra +
      • Matrices & Systems:
        • Gauss elimination, linear independence
        • Matrix rank, vector spaces
        • Solutions: Existence and uniqueness
      • Determinants & Solutions:
        • Cramer's rule, Gauss-Jordan elimination
      • Eigenproblems:
        • Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
        • Special matrices: Symmetric, skew-symmetric, orthogonal
        • Eigenbases, diagonalization
      • Advanced Topics:
        • Quadratic forms
        • Cayley-Hamilton theorem
    • Unit 4: Vector Calculus +
      • Vector Functions:
        • Scalar/vector fields, derivatives
        • Curves: Arc length, curvature, torsion
      • Differential Operators:
        • Gradient, directional derivative
        • Divergence, curl
      • Integration Theorems:
        • Line integrals, path independence
        • Double integrals, Green's theorem
        • Surface integrals, Stokes' theorem
        • Triple integrals, Divergence theorem
  • Environmental Science +
    • Unit 1: Fundamentals of Environment +
      • Introduction:
        • Multidisciplinary nature: Definition, scope, importance
        • Need for public awareness
      • Natural Resources & Ecosystems:
        • Concept, structure, and function
        • Types: Forest, aquatic, grassland
        • Biogeochemical cycles (C, N, P, H2O)
      • Biodiversity:
        • Biogeographical classification
        • India as mega-diversity nation
        • Endangered/endemic species
        • Threats & conservation
        • Bioprospecting vs biopiracy
    • Unit 2: Environmental Pollution +
      • Air Pollution:
        • Sources (industrial, vehicular)
        • Effects on biosphere & meteorology
        • Control measures (scrubbers, filters)
      • Water Pollution:
        • Sources (agricultural, industrial)
        • Types (organic, inorganic)
      • Soil Pollution: Causes & remediation
      • Other Pollutions:
        • Noise: Effects & control
        • Thermal & radiation
      • Waste Management:
        • Solid waste strategies (3Rs)
        • Pollution prevention
        • Disaster management
    • Unit 3: Social Issues & Environment +
      • Sustainable Development: Concepts & goals
      • Urban Challenges:
        • Energy issues
        • Water conservation
      • Land Management:
        • Wasteland reclamation
        • Resettlement & rehabilitation
      • Global Issues:
        • Climate change
        • Nuclear accidents
      • Policy & Assessment:
        • Environmental laws (wildlife, forests)
        • EIA process
        • Consumerism & waste
    • Unit 4: Human Dimensions +
      • Population Dynamics:
        • Growth trends
        • Family welfare programs
      • Health & Rights:
        • Environment-health linkages
        • HIV/AIDS awareness
        • Human rights
      • Social Welfare:
        • Women & child welfare
      • Technology Role: IT in environmental management
